Overview

My journey to achieving the Splunk Certified Cyber Defense Analyst Certification has been a transformative experience. Having completed 10 out of the 15 required courses, I have gained critical insights into the processes and thought patterns of a Cyber Defense Analyst, particularly in threat detection, analysis, and response. As a Cyber Defense Analyst in training, I am actively learning and practicing a broad array of frameworks, attack surfaces, and defense methodologies. My focus is on building a strong foundation in these concepts to better understand and mitigate cyber threats effectively. Below is a detailed outline of my current knowledge and progress in these areas.

Key Frameworks and Standards

Familiarity with cybersecurity frameworks is critical for understanding organizational security policies and threat modeling. I am currently building my understanding of the following frameworks:

  • CVE Program: Learning to prioritize mitigation efforts based on common vulnerabilities and exposures.
  • MITRE ATT&CK Enterprise Matrix: Studying adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) to improve threat identification.
  • Pyramid of Pain: Exploring how targeting different types of adversary data can aid in effective threat detection.
  • Lockheed Martin Cyber Kill Chain: Understanding the phases of a cyberattack to enhance detection and response strategies.
  • EU-GDPR, HIPAA, PCI-DSS: Gaining awareness of regulatory compliance and secure data handling practices.
  • ISO 27000 Series and NIST CSF/RMF: Learning guidelines for managing security risks and establishing controls.
  • OWASP Top Ten: Becoming familiar with common web application vulnerabilities like SQL injection and XSS.

Understanding and Analyzing Attack Surfaces

By exploring various attack surfaces, I am learning to anticipate and mitigate potential threats. My studies have included:

  • Phishing and Typesquatting: Understanding deceptive tactics used to steal credentials and sensitive information.
  • DNS Attacks: Gaining knowledge of tunneling, amplification, flooding, and spoofing techniques to defend against DNS abuse.
  • Malware and Ransomware: Learning to analyze payload behavior and defenses against spyware, adware, viruses, and trojans.
  • Supply Chain Attacks: Investigating vulnerabilities in third-party integrations to safeguard assets.
  • Indicators of Compromise (IOCs): Exploring how IOCs are used to detect breaches and malicious activities proactively.

Core Defense Analyst Skills

My growing technical understanding and analytical abilities form the basis of my defense strategies. Key skills I am working to develop include:

  • TTP Identification: Learning to recognize adversary tactics, techniques, and procedures to anticipate and counter attacks.
  • SIEM Technologies: Building proficiency in leveraging Splunk Enterprise Security to analyze logs and generate actionable insights.
  • SOC Structure: Understanding the roles and workflows within a Security Operations Center (SOC) to streamline incident response.

Conclusion

While I am still in the process of mastering these frameworks and techniques, my dedication to learning and practicing these fundamentals ensures continuous growth in my abilities as a Cyber Defense Analyst. I aim to further develop my understanding to contribute effectively to the security of organizational infrastructures.
